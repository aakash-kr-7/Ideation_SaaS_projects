## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to the ShouldBuild website, accounts, research tools, reports, subscriptions, billing integrations, exports, support communications, and related services.

It does not govern third-party websites linked in research reports or the independent privacy practices of third-party providers.

## 2. Information we collect

### A. Account and profile information

When you create or use an account, we may collect:

- name;
- email address;
- profile image;
- authentication provider;
- account identifiers;
- onboarding answers, preferences, and team membership;
- verification, login, and account-status information.

Authentication is provided through Supabase Auth. If you use Google sign-in, Google provides the profile information you authorize, typically your name, email address, profile image, and account identifier.

We do not receive your Google password. Password authentication credentials are handled by Supabase Auth.

### B. User Content and research data

We collect the information you submit to create projects and reports, including:

- product or business ideas;
- descriptions, target audiences, markets, locations, pricing assumptions, and constraints;
- research requests and follow-up inputs;
- project names and notes;
- generated evidence, scores, reports, citations, comparisons, and exports;
- feedback you provide about reports.

Do not submit highly sensitive personal data, trade secrets, confidential client data, unpublished patent details, passwords, or information you are not authorized to disclose.

### C. Usage and technical information

We may automatically collect:

- IP address;
- browser, device, and operating-system information;
- pages and features used;
- dates, times, session and referral information;
- research-run status, provider usage, errors, performance, and security logs;
- approximate location inferred from IP address;
- cookies or similar identifiers needed for authentication, preferences, security, and billing.

At launch, ShouldBuild uses necessary cookies and similar technologies required to operate accounts and the Service. If we later add non-essential analytics, advertising, or marketing cookies, we will update this Policy and provide consent controls where required.

### D. Payment and transaction information

Payments are handled by Dodo Payments, acting as Merchant of Record.

Dodo Payments may collect payment-card, wallet, UPI, bank, billing-address, tax, fraud-prevention, and identity information directly from you. ShouldBuild does not receive complete payment-card numbers.

We may receive transaction information needed to provide the Service, such as:

- customer and transaction identifiers;
- name and email;
- billing country;
- product, plan, amount, currency, and tax status;
- subscription, renewal, cancellation, refund, dispute, and payment status;
- invoice or receipt references.

### E. Communications

If you contact us, we collect the information in your message, your contact details, attachments you choose to send, and our response history.

## 3. How we use information

We use personal information to:

- create and secure accounts;
- authenticate users and maintain sessions;
- process research requests and generate reports;
- search, extract, normalize, score, and explain public evidence;
- provide citations, comparisons, exports, and report history;
- process purchases, subscriptions, credits, cancellations, and refunds;
- prevent fraud, abuse, duplicate free accounts, and security threats;
- enforce plan limits, acceptable-use rules, and legal terms;
- diagnose failures and improve reliability, relevance, and usability;
- respond to support and privacy requests;
- send essential account, security, billing, and service messages;
- comply with legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, and dispute obligations.

We do not sell personal information. We do not use User Content for targeted advertising.

## 4. Legal bases where applicable

Where laws such as the GDPR or UK GDPR apply, we process information based on one or more of the following:

- **Contract:** to create your account, process research, deliver reports, and administer purchases.
- **Legitimate interests:** to secure, operate, debug, improve, and prevent abuse of the Service, where those interests are not overridden by your rights.
- **Consent:** where you choose to receive optional communications or where consent is required for non-essential cookies.
- **Legal obligation:** to meet tax, accounting, consumer-protection, fraud-prevention, dispute, and regulatory duties.
- **Legal claims:** to establish, exercise, or defend legal rights.

## 5. How research data is processed

ShouldBuild uses external service providers to perform parts of the research pipeline.

Depending on the report and available providers, idea descriptions, queries, extracted public-source content, structured evidence, and limited report context may be processed by providers such as:

- Tavily for web search;
- Firecrawl for webpage extraction;
- Groq and Cerebras for structured AI reasoning;
- Cohere for embeddings and semantic comparison.

We aim to send only information reasonably necessary for the relevant task. These providers process data under their own agreements and privacy practices.

Because User Content may be transmitted to external providers, you should not use ShouldBuild as a confidential data room or submit information that requires a special data-processing agreement unless we have expressly agreed to one in writing.

## 8. Data retention

We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy.

Our intended retention periods are:

- account, project, report, and preference data: while your account remains active or until you delete the relevant content;
- deleted account and project data: removed from active systems within 30 days where reasonably possible;
- encrypted backups: may persist for up to 90 additional days before automatic deletion;
- security, usage, provider, and error logs: generally up to 12 months;
- support communications: generally up to 24 months;
- transaction, tax, invoice, refund, and dispute records: for the period required by Dodo Payments and applicable legal, tax, accounting, and compliance obligations.

We may retain information longer when necessary to investigate fraud, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, protect security, or comply with law.
